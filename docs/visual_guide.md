# Visual guide

This guide describes how to read each FluGenome3D view without overinterpreting it.

## Home / Overview

What to look for: the project framing, safe data badges, navigation path and claim boundaries.

What not to infer: that FluGenome3D is a commercial forecasting product or biological predictor.

## Dataset Atlas

What to look for: coverage, subtype/segment balance, geography, year bins and QC status.

What not to infer: global surveillance representativeness or sampling-unbiased prevalence.

## Sequence/Token Inspector

What to look for: composition summaries, CpG/UpA observed/expected, entropy, tokenizer behavior and codon/k-mer summaries.

What not to infer: biological causality, immune relevance, pathogenicity, fitness or antigenic interpretation.

## AntigenLM Latent Atlas

What to look for: descriptive organization of derived embeddings, coarse neighborhoods, outliers and subtype/year separation.

What not to infer: validated antigenic distance, antigenic drift, immune escape or vaccine relevance.

## Representation Projector

What to look for: how reduced-coordinate maps organize safe derived artifacts across feature families.

What not to infer: causal biological structure or predictive performance unless separately validated.

## 3D Molecular Viewer

What to look for: public structure context, chain selection, alignment coverage and mapping-QC status.

What not to infer: residue-level biological effect, escape, fitness or antigenic function unless independently validated.

## Bridge View

What to look for: how sequence summaries, representation neighborhoods and public structure references can be inspected together.

What not to infer: that visual proximity alone proves biological similarity or functional relatedness.

## Ask FluGenome3D

What to look for: plain-language explanations grounded in safe project docs and exported summaries.

What not to infer: that the guide has raw sequence access, external model access or biological prediction capability.
