# Claims and limitations

FluGenome3D is a scientific portfolio artifact and reproducibility companion. It is intentionally framed as descriptive, exploratory and governance-aware.

## Allowed claims

- FluGenome3D provides descriptive exploration of derived HA/NA artifacts.
- It audits sequence-context summaries, deterministic tokenization behavior and representation geometry.
- PCA and t-SNE maps are safe reduced-coordinate views for inspection.
- AntigenLM-derived embeddings are shown as descriptive latent representation geometry.
- Structure views use public PDB entries and conservative alignment-QC status.
- CDS-dependent summaries are limited to records passing documented CDS/QC filters.
- The deployed app is designed to avoid raw sequence, accession or isolate-name redistribution.

## Disallowed claims

- FluGenome3D predicts antigenic drift.
- FluGenome3D identifies immune escape mutations.
- FluGenome3D recommends vaccine candidates.
- FluGenome3D predicts fitness, pathogenicity, transmissibility or selection.
- FluGenome3D validates AntigenLM, GROVER, BPE or any tokenizer as biologically causal.
- FluGenome3D provides viral design or sequence optimization guidance.
- Structure mapping identifies antigenic residues or functional sites without independent validation.

## Interpretation boundaries

The app should be interpreted as a geometric audit, latent representation analysis and research visualization lab.

Visual proximity is not biological validation. Token enrichment is not immune relevance. Structure alignment QC is not residue-level functional evidence. CpG/UpA summaries are descriptive sequence-context features, not causal mechanisms.

## In-app guide boundary

`Ask FluGenome3D` is a grounded explanatory interface over safe docs, reports, formula cards and exported summaries. It does not access raw sequences, does not call an external model, and does not convert descriptive summaries into prediction or causality.
