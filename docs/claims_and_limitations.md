# Claims and limitations

## Permitted claims

- Reproducible descriptive audit.
- Sequence-context analysis of Influenza A HA/NA.
- Codon usage and dinucleotide bias summaries.
- Tokenizer-dependent representation audit inspired by DNA language models.
- Lightweight structure-aware visualization of descriptive metrics.
- Local GISAID-backed analysis with non-redistributable raw data.
- Public demo mode where possible.
- Grounded plain-language explanations from safe docs, reports and exported summaries.

## Prohibited claims

- Predicts antigenic drift.
- Predicts vaccine candidates.
- Identifies escape mutations.
- Predicts pathogenicity, transmissibility or fitness.
- GROVER understands Influenza A.
- Tokenization reveals functional sites.
- CpG/UpA explains pathogenicity.
- Structure mapping identifies antigenic residues.
- The in-app guide is an autonomous biological predictor.
- The in-app guide can access raw sequences or restricted local panels.

## Interpretation warning

All structure mappings are exploratory projections of descriptive sequence metrics. They are not mechanistic validation, antigenic interpretation, vaccine design, or functional site discovery.

The `Ask FluGenome3D` guide is an interpretive layer over safe artifacts. It can explain project concepts, formulas, views and governance, but it must not convert descriptive summaries into prediction, causality or biological validation.
